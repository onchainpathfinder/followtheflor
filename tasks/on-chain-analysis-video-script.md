## [SECTION 1: WHAT IS IT — 0:35–1:15]

**FLOR:**
"So what is on-chain analysis? Simple. It's taking raw blockchain data — wallet balances, transactions, fund flows — and turning it into actual intelligence."

"Think about it. In traditional banking, you can't see anyone's bank balance. You can't see where they sent money or when. That's all private."

"On the blockchain? It's all public. Every wallet's holdings, every transfer, every timestamp. On-chain analysis is the practice of reading that data and making sense of it."

"It boils down to two things:"

*[HOLD UP ONE FINGER]*
"One — what does a wallet HOLD?"

*[HOLD UP TWO FINGERS]*
"Two — where are funds MOVING?"

"Everything else — exchange flows, whale alerts, network health — it all builds on those two."

---

## [SECTION 2: WALLET TRACKING — 1:15–2:00]

**FLOR:**
"Let's start with wallet holdings. Every wallet address on a public blockchain has a visible balance. You can see exactly how much ETH, Bitcoin, or any token someone holds — right now and at any point in the past."

"Now here's where it gets interesting. Tools like Arkham use AI to link anonymous wallet addresses to real people and organisations. So it's not just 'wallet 0xB63A holds 2.6 million dollars.' It's 'this is likely Donald Trump's wallet and here's what's in it.'"

*[B-ROLL: Show Arkham entity page or similar — wallet with labelled entity]*

"Why does this matter? Because now you can VERIFY what people claim."

"A VC says they're 'long-term bullish' on a project? Check their wallet. Are they holding or dumping? An influencer promotes a token? Check if they actually own it — or if they're being paid to shill."

"Don't trust. Verify. The blockchain lets you do that."

---

## [SECTION 3: EXCHANGE FLOWS — 2:00–2:50]

**FLOR:**
"Now, my favourite part: exchange flows."

"This tracks movement between personal wallets and exchange wallets. The logic is simple:"

*[GESTURE — pointing one direction]*
"Crypto moving TO an exchange? Probably preparing to sell. Bearish."

*[GESTURE — pointing opposite direction]*
"Crypto moving OUT of an exchange? Taking custody, probably holding. Bullish."

"At scale, these flows reveal market sentiment BEFORE prices move. In 2022, massive stablecoin inflows to exchanges showed people cashing out weeks before the crash was obvious."

*[LEAN IN — compliance angle]*

"Now from my world — compliance — exchange flows are critical. A wallet with no history suddenly sending a large amount to an exchange? That's a red flag. Under MiCA, if you're running a VASP, monitoring these inbound flows is literally your regulatory obligation."

"Same data. Different use case. Traders use it to make money. Compliance uses it to catch bad actors."

---

## [SECTION 4: WHALE ALERTS + ETFs — 2:50–3:50]

**FLOR:**
"You've probably heard of 'whales' — wallets holding massive amounts of crypto. When a whale moves, the market pays attention."

"Real example: a Bitcoin wallet that hadn't moved in TEN YEARS suddenly transferred 400 BTC to Binance. That's over 10 million dollars at the time. On-chain analysts caught it in real time. The rest of the market? Found out later, when the price was already dropping."

"You can set up alerts for this. Pick a wallet, set a threshold, get notified the second something moves. It's like having a surveillance system for the blockchain."

*[TRANSITION]*

"And it's not just anonymous whales anymore. Since the US approved spot Bitcoin ETFs in 2024, institutional wallets are visible on-chain too."

"BlackRock's IBIT, Fidelity's FBTC — you can see their Bitcoin accumulation in near real-time. No waiting for quarterly filings. The blockchain tells you what Wall Street is doing TODAY."

"For compliance professionals: this is a transparency revolution. Corporate crypto claims can now be independently verified, on-chain, by anyone."

---

## [SECTION 5: ON-CHAIN VS TECHNICAL — 3:50–4:20]

**FLOR:**
"Quick distinction because people always ask."

"Technical analysis looks at PRICE CHARTS — candles, moving averages, patterns. It tries to predict where price goes based on where it's been. It uses zero blockchain data."

"On-chain analysis looks at what's ACTUALLY HAPPENING — who's buying, who's selling, where money is flowing. It doesn't predict price directly. It reveals the behaviours driving it."

*[HAND GESTURE — weighing both]*

"A technical analyst sees a resistance level on a chart. An on-chain analyst sees three whale wallets just moved 50,000 ETH to Coinbase. Both tell you something. On-chain tells you WHY it's about to happen."

"They're complementary. Smart people use both."
