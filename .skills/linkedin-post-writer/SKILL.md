## Overview

This skill writes LinkedIn posts for Floriana's personal profile, promoting floronchain educational content. LinkedIn is floronchain's secondary distribution channel — where compliance professionals, crypto founders, and regulators engage with professional content.

**Critical context:** Flor's boss and colleagues are on LinkedIn. Every post must read as professional development content, not personal branding. "Here's what MiCA means for crypto exchanges" is fine. "I'm building my side project" is not.

---

## How LinkedIn Works (So Your Posts Perform)

- **First 2-3 lines** are visible before "...see more" — this is your hook. If they don't click, they don't read.
- **Dwell time** matters — LinkedIn's algorithm rewards posts people spend time reading
- **Comments in the first hour** determine reach. Ask genuine questions that experts want to answer.
- **Native content > links** — LinkedIn deprioritises posts with external links. If linking to an article, put the link in the first comment, not the post.
- **Document/carousel posts** get high engagement on LinkedIn (PDF carousels)
- **Ideal length:** 150-200 words. Not shorter (too thin), not longer (loses attention).
- **Post frequency:** 2-3x per week maximum. Quality over quantity on LinkedIn.
- **Best times:** Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am CET (professionals checking LinkedIn at work)

```
[BODY — 3-5 short paragraphs]
One idea per paragraph.
Short sentences.
White space between paragraphs.
Concrete examples and numbers.

[CTA — last line]
A question that invites expert discussion.
NOT "Follow me for more" — that's for influencers, not professionals.

```

## Voice Rules (Floriana on LinkedIn)

- **Professional and educational.** This is an expert sharing knowledge, not building a brand.
- **Authoritative.** Flor speaks from experience — "In my work with iGaming compliance..." is fine.
- **Measured.** Less spicy than X/Twitter. Strong opinions are fine, but framed professionally.
- **No personal branding energy.** No "I'm building..." or "My journey..." or "I recently launched..."
- **Reference the topic, not the platform.** Don't say "As I wrote on floronchain" — say "The MiCA stablecoin rules mean..." and put the link in the comments.

### NEVER:
- "I'm excited to share..."
- "Thrilled to announce..."
- "Here are my thoughts on..." (just give the thoughts)
- Self-promotional language about floronchain
- Hashtag stuffing (3 max, at the bottom)
- Posts that would make Flor's boss uncomfortable

### ALWAYS:
- Lead with the insight or value
- Frame as professional education ("Here's what practitioners need to know")
- Include specific regulatory references or data
- End with a question that compliance professionals or founders want to discuss
- Keep it under 200 words

## The Link Strategy

LinkedIn deprioritises posts with external links. To work around this:

1. Write the post as a standalone insight (it should be valuable even without clicking through)
2. End with: "Full breakdown in the comments" or similar
3. Post the floronchain.com article link as the **first comment**

This way the post gets algorithmic reach AND drives traffic to the article.
