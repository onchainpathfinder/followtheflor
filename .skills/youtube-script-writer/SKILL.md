## Overview

This skill writes video scripts for the **Flor Onchain** YouTube channel. The channel primarily covers crypto compliance, iGaming regulation, and AI in compliance — with occasional Malta lifestyle crossover content that humanises the brand.

YouTube is floronchain's AUTHORITY platform. Videos have a long shelf life — a good MiCA explainer will get views for years. Every script should be the kind of content someone bookmarks and rewatches.

---

## How YouTube Works (So Your Scripts Perform)

- **Title + thumbnail** determine click-through rate (CTR). If nobody clicks, the video dies.
- **First 30 seconds** determine retention. YouTube measures how many viewers stay — if they leave early, the algorithm stops recommending.
- **Watch time is the #1 metric.** A 7-minute video where people watch 6 minutes outperforms a 15-minute video where people leave at minute 3.
- **Educational content** has massive long-tail potential on YouTube — people search for "what is MiCA" for years.
- **Optimal video length:** 7-12 minutes for explainers (long enough for mid-roll ads, short enough to retain). 3-5 minutes for quick explainers.
- **Consistency:** 1 video per week minimum to build momentum. Even biweekly works if quality is high.
- **SEO matters on YouTube** — title, description, tags, and what you say in the first 60 seconds all affect search ranking.

## Script Format

### Long-form Explainer (7-12 minutes)

```
[HOOK — 0:00-0:30]
PURPOSE: Stop them from clicking away. Address their problem/question directly.
VISUAL: Talking head OR striking B-roll of Malta/screen recording
SCRIPT: Start with the question the viewer has. "If you're running a crypto exchange in the EU, you need to understand MiCA. Here's what actually matters."
DO NOT: Start with "Hey guys, welcome to my channel" — that's what everyone does and it loses viewers.

[CONTEXT — 0:30-1:30]
PURPOSE: Set up why this matters right now. Create urgency.
VISUAL: Mix of talking head + on-screen text/graphics
SCRIPT: The background. Why now. Who's affected. What's at stake.

[MAIN CONTENT — 1:30-8:00]
PURPOSE: The substance. This is why they clicked.
STRUCTURE: 3-5 clear sections, each with a mini-hook.
VISUAL: Talking head, screen shares, on-screen bullet points, document highlights
SCRIPT: One section at a time. Each section should feel complete but build on the last.
TRANSITIONS: "Now here's where it gets interesting..." / "But that's not the full story..." / "The part most people miss..."

[FLOR'S TAKE — 8:00-9:00]
PURPOSE: The opinion that makes this Flor's video, not Wikipedia.
VISUAL: Talking head, maybe Malta background
SCRIPT: "Here's what I actually think about this..." — honest, direct, possibly spicy.

[CTA — 9:00-9:30]
PURPOSE: Drive subscriptions and traffic.
VISUAL: Talking head
SCRIPT: "If this was useful, subscribe — I break down crypto regulation every week. Full written guide at floronchain.com. And if you want to go deeper on [related topic], watch this next."
END SCREEN: Link to related video + subscribe button
```

### Quick Explainer (3-5 minutes)

```
[HOOK — 0:00-0:15]
One question, one bold statement. No preamble.
"What is the Travel Rule and why should crypto exchanges care? 3 minutes."

[EXPLANATION — 0:15-3:30]
Clear, structured explanation. Use analogies for complex concepts.
On-screen text for key terms and numbers.
Keep it moving — no tangents.

[SO WHAT — 3:30-4:00]
What this means in practice. Who's affected. What to do.

[CTA — 4:00-4:30]
Subscribe, link to full article, suggest next video.
```

```
What connects Malta to the professional life.

```

## Voice Rules (Flor Onchain on YouTube)

- **Confident and clear.** Speak like you're explaining to a smart colleague, not lecturing a classroom.
- **Direct to camera** is the primary style. Eye contact builds trust.
- **Conversational pace.** Not too fast (people need to absorb complex topics), not too slow (they'll click away).
- **Use "you" often.** "Here's what you need to know" > "Let me explain what this means."
- **Analogies for complex concepts.** "Think of MiCA like a driving licence for crypto companies in Europe."

### NEVER:
- "Hey guys, don't forget to like and subscribe" at the beginning (put CTA at the end)
- Read regulation text word-for-word on screen
- Talk for more than 60 seconds without a visual change
- Use jargon without immediate explanation
- Make promises you can't keep ("This video will make you an expert")

### ALWAYS:
- Hook within the first 10 seconds
- On-screen text for every key term, number, or date
- Change the visual every 15-30 seconds (cut to B-roll, screen share, text overlay)
- Include Flor's personal take — not just information delivery
- Suggest a specific next video at the end (retention strategy)

## Title & Thumbnail Suggestions

Include with every script:

**Title rules:**
- Under 60 characters
- Front-load the keyword ("MiCA Explained" not "An Explanation of MiCA")
- Promise something specific ("in 7 minutes", "what you need to know", "the complete guide")
- Use numbers where possible ("5 Things", "3 Red Flags")

**Thumbnail rules:**
- Bold text (3-5 words max)
- Flor's face with expression (curiosity, concern, confidence)
- High contrast colours
- Malta background when relevant (distinctive, memorable)

## Quality Standard

A perfect script:
- Hook in the first 10 seconds that addresses the viewer's question/problem
- Teaches something specific that the viewer can use
- Flor's personality comes through — not a corporate presentation
- Paced well — no section drags
- Visual variety noted throughout (not just talking head for 10 minutes)
- SEO-ready metadata included
- Someone would subscribe after watching this

A failed script:
- Starts with "Hey guys, welcome back to my channel"
- Reads like a regulation document out loud
- No personality, no take, no Flor
- Over 12 minutes with content that could fit in 7
- No visual change suggestions
- Missing SEO metadata
