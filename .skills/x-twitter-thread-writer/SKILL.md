## Overview

This skill writes X/Twitter content for @floronchain — Flor's crypto compliance and regulation brand. X is where the crypto compliance conversation actually happens: compliance professionals, crypto founders, regulators, degens, and journalists all coexist here.

X/Twitter is floronchain's PRIMARY distribution channel. Every thread should build Flor's reputation as the compliance expert who makes regulation make sense.

---

## How X/Twitter Works (So Your Content Performs)

- **Thread hook (Tweet 1)** determines whether anyone reads the rest. If the hook fails, the thread fails.
- **Engagement in the first 30 minutes** determines reach — post when your audience is online
- **Quote tweets with your own take** get more engagement than plain retweets
- **Threads that teach something specific** outperform opinion threads
- **The "bookmark bait" effect:** If people save your thread, the algorithm pushes it harder
- **Optimal thread length:** 5-8 tweets for standard threads, up to 12 for major regulation breakdowns
- **Best posting times for crypto/compliance:** Weekday mornings (8-10am CET), early afternoon (1-3pm CET)
- **Engagement farming ("Agree?", "Thoughts?") is dead** — X users see through it. Ask genuine questions.

## Content Types

### 1. Thread (5-8 tweets)
The main content format. Takes an article, regulation, or insight and breaks it into a digestible thread.

```
Tweet 1: [HOOK]
Bold claim, surprising stat, provocative question, or news peg.
Must stop the scroll. Must make someone think "I need to read this."

Tweet 2-6: [KEY POINTS]
One idea per tweet. Each tweet should stand alone but build on the last.
Use specific numbers, examples, and regulatory references.
Short paragraphs. White space. Easy to scan.

Tweet 7: [FLOR'S TAKE]
The opinion. This is what makes it Flor's, not a Wikipedia summary.
"Here's what I actually think about this."

Tweet 8: [CTA]
"Full breakdown: [link]" or "Subscribe to the newsletter for more: [link]"
Or a question that drives genuine replies.
```

### 2. Single Tweet (Hot Take)
Quick reaction to news, a bold statement, or a standalone insight.

- **Max impact in 280 characters**
- Must be quotable — people should want to QT this with "THIS"
- Strong opinion > lukewarm observation
- Can include an image, chart, or screenshot for context

### 3. Quote Tweet (with analysis)
When regulatory news drops or another account posts something relevant.

- **Add value, don't just agree** — "This is huge" adds nothing. "Here's what this actually means for exchanges operating in the EU" adds value.
- Keep it to 2-3 sentences max
- Link the insight to practical impact

### 4. News Reaction Thread (3-5 tweets)
Faster, shorter thread when news breaks.

```
Tweet 1: The news + why it matters (with link/screenshot)
Tweet 2-3: What this means in practice
Tweet 4: Flor's take
Tweet 5: CTA or question
```

## Voice Rules (floronchain on X)

- **Professional but human.** Bloomberg meets crypto Twitter.
- **Direct and opinionated.** No "on the other hand" hedging unless both sides genuinely have merit.
- **Expertise-first.** Flor speaks as someone who does this for a living, not someone who read about it.
- **Accessible.** Explain jargon. Don't assume everyone knows what RahaPTS is.
- **Occasionally spicy.** A well-placed strong take gets engagement and builds reputation.

### NEVER:
- "Not financial advice" on every tweet (save the disclaimer for articles)
- Engagement farming: "Drop a [emoji] if you agree"
- Vague takes: "This will be interesting to watch" (say WHY)
- Panic-posting: "CRYPTO IS ABOUT TO BE BANNED" clickbait
- Starting with "Thread:" or "THREAD [emoji]" (outdated, looks amateur)

### ALWAYS:
- Specific regulatory references (name the directive, cite the article)
- Practical impact ("If you're running an exchange, this means you need to...")
- Flor's honest opinion somewhere in the thread
- A reason for someone to bookmark/save the thread

## Hashtag/Keyword Strategy

X is less hashtag-dependent than Instagram, but strategic use helps discovery:

- **1-2 hashtags max** (more looks spammy on X)
- Use them in the last tweet of a thread, not the hook
- **Useful hashtags:** #MiCA #AML #CryptoCompliance #FATF #AMLR
- **Mention relevant accounts** when appropriate (@ABORAESMA, @FATFNews) — can drive QTs from official accounts

## Quality Standard

A perfect thread:
- Hook makes a compliance officer or crypto founder stop scrolling
- Each tweet teaches something specific — no filler tweets
- Includes Flor's genuine perspective, not just a summary
- Someone would bookmark it as a reference
- Drives at least one type of meaningful engagement (bookmark, QT, thoughtful reply)

A failed thread:
- Reads like a Wikipedia summary of a regulation
- Could have been written by anyone with access to Google
- Has filler tweets ("Let me explain..." "Are you still with me?")
- No opinion, no take, no personality
- Over 10 tweets with nothing to cut
