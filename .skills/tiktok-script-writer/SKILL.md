## Overview

This skill writes short-form video scripts (15-60 seconds) for TikTok and Instagram Reels under the @followtheflor brand. The content covers running/fitness and Malta/Gozo exploration — raw, real, and visual.

TikTok is followtheflor's GROWTH platform. The algorithm rewards authentic, niche content — which is exactly what Flor has. A woman in her 40s running Mediterranean cliffs, discovering hidden beaches, and living the real Malta life is a genuinely underserved niche.

---

## How TikTok Works (So Your Scripts Perform)

- **Hook in the first 1-2 seconds** — if they don't stop scrolling, nothing else matters
- **Retention is king** — TikTok measures what percentage of the video people watch. Keep them watching until the end.
- **Text on screen** is essential — most people watch without sound initially
- **Trending sounds** boost reach — note where a trending sound could be added
- **Native feel** beats polished production — phone camera, natural light, real locations
- **15-30 seconds** is the sweet spot for most content. Only go to 60 if the content demands it.
- **Post 1-2x daily** for growth, minimum 3-4x per week to stay in the algorithm

---

## Script Format

Every script follows this structure:

```
[HOOK — 1-2 seconds]
TEXT ON SCREEN: "Bold text that stops the scroll"
VISUAL: What the viewer sees
AUDIO: Voiceover / trending sound / natural audio

[BODY — 10-40 seconds]
TEXT ON SCREEN: Key points (one per scene change)
VISUAL: Shot-by-shot description
AUDIO: Voiceover script OR sound suggestion
TRANSITIONS: Cut type if relevant

[PAYOFF — 2-5 seconds]
TEXT ON SCREEN: Final punchline or CTA
VISUAL: The reveal, the view, the moment
AUDIO: Music swell / final voiceover line

[CAPTION]
Short caption (1-2 lines max for TikTok)
3-5 hashtags
```

## Voice Rules (followtheflor on TikTok)

- **Talking to camera is fine** — in fact it's preferred. Flor's face builds connection.
- **Voiceover style:** Casual, like telling a friend. Not scripted-sounding.
- **Text on screen:** Short, bold, punchy. 5-7 words max per text overlay.
- **Show, don't tell:** The video should show the run, the beach, the climb — voiceover adds context.
- **Raw over polished:** Shaky phone footage from a cliff top > studio-quality B-roll
- **Personality:** Confident, slightly sarcastic, real. "This is what 40 looks like after 8km in July heat" energy.

### NEVER:
- Start with "Hey guys!" or "So today I'm going to..."
- Use generic transitions for no reason
- Make it look like an ad
- Over-explain — TikTok audiences are smart and impatient

### ALWAYS:
- Hook with something unexpected or visually striking
- Include text on screen for every key moment
- End with something that makes people comment, share, or watch again
- Note where trending sounds could be inserted

## Shooting Tips (Include When Relevant)

When suggesting a script, add practical filming advice:

- **Golden hour** (sunrise/sunset) makes everything look cinematic
- **Stabilise with two hands** or use a cheap phone gimbal
- **Film in 9:16** (vertical, full screen)
- **Multiple angles** even with one phone — wide, close-up, POV
- **B-roll is free** — film everything, you'll use it in edits
- **CapCut** for editing — auto-captions, transitions, speed ramps
- **Film the journey, not just the destination** — the walk down to the beach IS content

## Quality Standard

A perfect script:
- Hook makes you stop scrolling in 1 second
- Text on screen carries the story even on mute
- Shows something specific to Flor and Malta — not generic fitness/travel content
- Has a payoff that makes people watch again or share
- Is filmable with a phone — no complex production required

A failed script:
- Sounds like every other fitness/travel TikTok
- Has no hook — starts slow
- Requires professional equipment or editing
- Is over 60 seconds with no justification
- Has no text on screen
