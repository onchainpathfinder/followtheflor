## How Newsletters Work (So Yours Gets Opened)

- **Subject line** determines open rate. If they don't open it, nothing else matters.
- **Preview text** (first line of email) is the second hook — it shows next to the subject line in most email clients.
- **First paragraph** determines whether they read or skim. Get to the value immediately.
- **One primary CTA per newsletter.** Don't overwhelm with 5 different links.
- **Consistency builds trust.** Same day, same time, every week. Subscribers expect it.
- **Open rates benchmark:** 40-50% is great for niche newsletters. Below 30% means your subject lines need work.
- **Keep it concise.** 300-500 words for the main body. Newsletter ≠ blog post. Tease the article, don't reproduce it.

## Newsletter Format

### Standard Issue (accompanies a new article)

```
SUBJECT LINE: [Compelling, specific, under 50 characters]
PREVIEW TEXT: [First line visible in inbox — continues the hook]

---

[PERSONAL OPENER — 2-3 sentences]
Something real. What Flor's been up to, what prompted this piece, a quick personal note.
This is what makes newsletters feel human, not automated.

[THE MAIN PIECE — 2-3 paragraphs]
The key insight from the article. Not a summary — a teaser.
Give enough value that the email alone is worth reading.
But leave enough that they want to click through to the full article.

[LINK TO FULL ARTICLE]
"Read the full breakdown: [title + link]"

[BONUS — 1-2 lines, optional]
A quick extra: something Flor read this week, a tool recommendation, a hot take in one sentence.

[SIGN-OFF]
Flor's voice. Brief. "See you next week." or "Allura, alla prossima."
```

### Standalone Issue (no article, just the newsletter)

```
SUBJECT LINE: [Compelling, specific]
PREVIEW TEXT: [Hook]

---

[OPENER — 2-3 sentences]
Personal, sets the tone.

[MAIN CONTENT — 3-5 short sections]
Could be: top 3 things from this week, a mini-essay, a curated list of links with commentary.
Each section has a bold header and 2-3 sentences.

[CTA]
"Reply to this email and tell me..." — replies boost deliverability AND build community.

[SIGN-OFF]
```

## Subject Line Rules

Subject lines make or break open rates. Rules:

- **Under 50 characters** (truncated on mobile otherwise)
- **Specific > vague.** "MiCA stablecoin rules: what changed" > "This week in crypto"
- **Curiosity gap works.** "The red flag everyone misses" > "Red flags in crypto"
- **Numbers work.** "3 things I learned running in 35°C" > "Running in the heat"
- **Don't clickbait.** The content must deliver what the subject line promises.
- **Test different styles:** Question, statement, number, challenge

**Examples:**
- followtheflor: "The bay nobody talks about" / "I skipped the run. Here's why." / "San Blas at 7am"
- floronchain: "MiCA is live. Now what?" / "The €32K rule most exchanges ignore" / "AI just changed transaction monitoring"

## Quality Standard

A perfect newsletter:
- Subject line makes someone open it immediately
- First paragraph delivers value or hooks curiosity
- Reads in under 3 minutes
- Has exactly one clear CTA
- Sounds like Flor — not a brand, not a robot
- Subscriber thinks "I'm glad I signed up for this"

A failed newsletter:
- Subject line is generic ("Weekly Update #12")
- Reproduces the full article instead of teasing it
- Over 500 words with no reason
- Multiple competing CTAs
- Sounds like it was written by an AI with no context (the irony is not lost on me)
- No personal touch — pure information delivery
