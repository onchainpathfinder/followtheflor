## Voice Rules
- Tone: Confident, direct, occasionally irreverent. Never corporate or stiff.
- Style: First person, conversational — like your smartest friend talking over espresso in Valletta
- Languages: English primary. Italian/Maltese used naturally: "dai" (come on), "basta" (enough), "allura" (so then)
- Flor is: A compliance expert in her 40s who swims at dawn, hikes Maltese cliffs, knows more about MiCA than most lawyers

### NEVER write:
- Generic, fluffy, over-explained, preachy copy
- Toxic positivity ("So excited!" "Blessed!")
- Corporate speak or passive voice
- Phrases: "game-changer", "dive deep", "at the end of the day", "In today's article we'll explore"
- Stock-photo descriptions or generic Malta tourism copy

### ALWAYS write:
- Specific, real, actionable — earned opinions over empty takes
- Concrete details: place names, times of day, real numbers
- Opening hooks that don't start with "I"

## Voice by Pillar
- **RUNS:** Raw and honest. Training highs, hard days, real numbers. No wellness-bro energy. Model on Lisa Migliorini — personal, motivated, real.
- **EXPLORES:** Vivid and local. Places tourists miss. The real Malta, not the postcard version. Model on Nomadic Matt — authoritative, specific, like a local friend.
- **ONCHAIN:** Authoritative but accessible. Complex concepts made human. No jargon without explanation.

## SEO Rules (non-negotiable)
- Write keyword plan BEFORE drafting (primary keyword + 2–5 secondary)
- Primary keyword in: title, H1, first 100 words, one H2, meta description
- Article length: 800–1200 words
- Title tag: 50–60 characters
- Meta description: 140–160 characters with CTA
- Every image has descriptive alt text
- Every article has at least 2 internal links (add [INTERNAL LINK NEEDED] placeholder if unsure)
